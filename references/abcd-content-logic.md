# ABCD Content Logic

Use this reference to turn an approved position and source base into a buyer-facing commercial material. ABCD controls the reader's path through the material. It does not replace the genre structure found in best-in-class examples, the source ledger or the production skill.

## Canon

Paper Planes uses four questions:

1. **Attention — узнаёт ли читатель свою ситуацию?** Name the role, moment, problem, consequence or desired progress in the reader's language. A company biography or generic competence claim is weak Attention.
2. **Benefits — понимает ли читатель, что изменится для него?** State the result in the client's process, behaviour, time, money, risk, control or quality. A list of supplier actions belongs to the solution mechanism until it is connected to a client result.
3. **Credentials — достаточно ли причин поверить именно этому обещанию?** Bind every important promise to relevant evidence: a comparable case, verified result, method, specialist, licence, equipment, documented process, review or other proof appropriate to the audience. Proof volume cannot compensate for weak relevance.
4. **Destination — понимает ли читатель следующий шаг и что произойдёт после него?** Name the recipient's action, the provider's response, required inputs, timing and output of the first step. Contacts or an isolated button are a weak Destination.

## Scope and order

Apply ABCD at three levels:

| Level | Required result |
| --- | --- |
| Whole material | The sequence lets the reader recognise the situation, see the value, trust the promise and take the next step. |
| Section or page group | Every substantial claim cluster has a reader relevance, benefit and matching proof. Destination appears where a decision or handoff is expected. |
| High-value page | The action title states the reader conclusion; the page contains the evidence or mechanism needed to support it and makes the intended response clear. |

ABCD is a presentation order, not a demand for exactly four pages or four labelled blocks. The complete best-in-class genre sequence remains authoritative. A one-page referral card may compress all four functions; a long proposal may repeat the cycle for several decisions.

## ABCD receipt before layout

Add these fields to every asset brief:

| Field | What to record |
| --- | --- |
| Reader and moment | Who reads, what has just happened, what decision is pending |
| Attention | Exact situation, tension, consequence or desired progress the reader recognises |
| Benefits | Observable client result and, when available, its measure or success criterion |
| Credentials | Claim-to-proof pairs with source, freshness, relevance and permission status |
| Destination | Reader action, provider response, inputs, timing and first-step output |
| Genre sequence | How the four functions appear inside the page order found in same-job references |
| Gaps | Missing fact, proof, permission or action detail that blocks client-ready status |

Do not mark the receipt complete with topic labels. Write the exact client-facing thesis or information object for every row.

## Claim-to-proof check

For every material promise, record:

```text
promise -> client benefit -> proof -> source and date -> qualification -> reader decision
```

Credentials fail when the proof is impressive but unrelated to the promise. A general review may support courtesy or service experience; it cannot prove a clinical outcome, delivery capacity or financial result unless its content and source directly support that claim.

For B2B, comparable cases, process evidence and verified results usually carry more weight than generic testimonials. For B2C and healthcare, reviews, licences, specialist qualifications, real facilities and documented patient routes may be strong evidence when they answer the reader's actual concern and respect privacy and medical-claim boundaries.

## Reader-facing evidence rule

Evidence pages speak to the recipient of the material. Show the evidence itself, explain what it proves and connect it to the reader's decision. An internal audit conclusion such as “we analysed reviews and found…” is suitable only when the material's job is to report that audit.

Example:

- weak for a patient brochure: a methodology note about how the team audited reviews;
- strong: selected verified quotations, named aspects of care they support, source/date and a careful explanation of what the reader may conclude;
- valid counterexample: a management report whose explicit job is to present the review audit and its method.

## Destination strength ladder

1. **Missing** — the material ends after information or price.
2. **Contact only** — phone, email, button or form with no expectation.
3. **Action stated** — the reader knows what to do.
4. **First step described** — action, response time, required input and first output are clear.
5. **Decision route shown** — the reader sees the first step, subsequent stages, responsibilities, timing and decision points appropriate to the material.

Use level 4 as the normal floor for a decision-oriented sales-support material. A small reminder card may legitimately use level 3 when its only job is to help an already-informed recipient perform a known action.

## Gate

Before `copy_ready`, answer with evidence:

- Can the intended reader recognise their situation in the opening or first relevant block?
- Are benefits stated as client outcomes and separated from supplier activity?
- Does every high-value promise have relevant, current and permitted proof?
- Does the material show what the reader does next and what follows?
- Does the page order follow same-job best-in-class references while preserving ABCD?
- Does each evidence page show recipient-facing proof instead of an internal production or audit note?
- Do action titles, captions, diagrams and calls to action use the client's vocabulary?

Any missing answer returns the asset to `copy_in_progress` or `blocked_by_source_gap`. Layout, imagery and art direction cannot repair a failed ABCD receipt.
