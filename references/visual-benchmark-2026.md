# Visual Benchmark 2026 for Client Sales Materials

## Why this gate exists

A file can be readable, branded and technically correct while still looking like a safe generated template. Client-facing commercial materials need a visible creative decision: an authored composition, specific imagery, a deliberate type hierarchy and a rhythm that matches each buyer moment.

Use current category references, best-in-class analogs and contemporary design signals as evidence. Dribbble and Behance provide discovery signals; live brand work, official websites, published decks and client sources provide stronger evidence for production choices.

## Required reference board

Before the first anchor asset, collect at least nine references:

- three direct category references;
- three analog references with comparable trust or product complexity;
- three current editorial, typographic, motion, photographic or interface references.

For every reference, record source URL, date checked, transferable mechanic, buyer fit, production consequence and the element excluded from transfer.

## 2026 Scorecard

Score each anchor asset from `1` to `5` on nine dimensions.

| Dimension | `1` | `3` | `5` |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Brand truth | generic colours and logo placement | recognisable brand application | brand codes become a distinctive, usable visual language |
| Composition | default split, centered card or unused canvas | hierarchy is clear | page tension, crop, scale and grid direct attention intentionally |
| Typography | generic hierarchy and mechanical wrapping | readable hierarchy | type carries tone, pacing and emphasis with deliberate line breaks and scale |
| Imagery | generic stock or weak generated scene | relevant high-quality image | specific, credible art direction that advances the buyer story |
| Visual idea | decoration supports text | coherent motif | a visual metaphor or proof mechanism makes the proposition memorable |
| Information design | labels and cards explain the content | diagram or table is usable | the object lets the reader compare, inspect or decide quickly |
| Rhythm | one composition repeats throughout | several formats appear | dense and quiet pages, photo-led and evidence-led pages alternate by purpose |
| Craft | basic alignment and contrast | clean rendered surface | image crop, spacing, layers, details and responsive behavior feel authored |
| Buyer and category fit | style could belong to any company | category is legible | visual choices make this company's competence and operating reality believable |

### Passing threshold

- `client_ready`: at least `36 / 45`, with no dimension below `3`;
- `art-direction ready`: at least `40 / 45`, with composition, imagery, typography and rhythm each at least `4`;
- below `36`: revise the anchor asset and direction before production expands.

The score is a review instrument. It does not convert taste into a fake objective fact. The receipt must name the evidence behind every score below `4`.

## Anti-template checks

Treat the following as a repair trigger:

- the logo could be replaced with another company's logo with little visual consequence;
- more than two neighbouring assets use the same composition, photograph treatment and card grammar;
- every slide is built as a title plus cards, even when a photo spread, editorial page, comparison, evidence crop, diagram or table would better serve the buyer;
- the visual treatment says "industrial" only through dark blue, red accents, hard hats or anonymous factory scenes;
- generated photos contain fake letterforms, inaccurate equipment, blurred key details or repeated people/scenes;
- a high-visibility image is displayed larger than its reliable resolution supports;
- a diagram repeats generic rounded boxes or connector lines without making a relationship inspectable;
- page numbers, scale marks, labels or grid lines act as decoration without supporting navigation or evidence.

## Image2 art-direction brief

Every high-visibility generated image requires a production card:

```markdown
Material and buyer role:
Scene and evidence object:
Location and industry reality:
People, PPE and equipment constraints:
Camera, light, weather, material and crop:
Brand treatment added outside the image:
Reference mechanics adopted:
Negative constraints:
Target dimensions and usage size:
```

Place wordmarks, exact logos, numbers and legal text as vector/HTML layers after generation. Do not depend on generated lettering for brand-critical content.

## Sequence review

Before client delivery, inspect:

1. the first, middle and last asset at intended viewing size;
2. a full contact sheet of the sequence;
3. the complete asset at 100% scale;
4. an image-resolution and crop check for every high-visibility visual;
5. one independent visual critique against the approved reference board.

Record:

```markdown
## Visual benchmark receipt

Assets reviewed:
Reference board:
Score by dimension:
Repeated grammar found:
Image and typography corrections:
Repair route:
Fresh render checked:
Resulting status:
```

## Improvement route

| Observation | Repair |
| --- | --- |
| Correct but generic visual language | return to `territory-choice` with stronger reference mechanics |
| Strong direction, weak individual page | revise the current asset with a composition or image change |
| Repetition across a batch | add a purpose-led layout type and rebuild the affected sequence |
| Weak generated image | regenerate from the art-direction brief or replace with verified client photography |
| Score remains below threshold after two revisions | request creative-direction review before more production |
