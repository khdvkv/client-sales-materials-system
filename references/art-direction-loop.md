# Art-Direction Loop

## Job

Create a client-specific visual language with the craft, relevance and memorability expected from a strong art-direction team. The loop treats visual quality as a sequence of evidence-backed creative decisions, not as a generic "modern" style request.

## 1. Reference Atlas

Build an atlas before generation. It contains twelve references:

- four direct category or buyer-context references;
- four analog references with comparable complexity, trust or proof burden;
- four current creative references selected for typography, photography, editorial rhythm, information design or motion.

Each row records:

| Field | Required content |
| --- | --- |
| Source | URL, screenshot or file; date checked |
| Creative author | studio, designer, photographer or brand when known |
| Context | category, channel, audience and viewing situation |
| Transfer unit | exact composition, type treatment, image logic, sequence device or information object |
| Adaptation | how the unit changes for this client and material |
| Exclusion | what would mislead, overpromise or copy the source too closely |
| Evidence | why the unit supports this buyer's trust or decision |

Use awards and curated work as a discovery layer, then verify the work on its original public surface where possible. Dribbble and Behance can surface artists and directions; they do not certify client fit or execution quality.

## 2. Creative Proposition

Write a proposition before layouts or prompts:

```text
For [buyer in situation], the material makes [commercial promise] believable by showing [specific proof] through [visual tension, material, camera, type and sequence rule].
```

It must name the physical or operational reality of the client. "Dark industrial", "premium", "modern" and similar descriptions are insufficient.

## 3. Divergence Set

Produce three territories from the same content. Each territory differs in at least two of these dimensions:

- visual metaphor or proof mechanism;
- image role and camera distance;
- type rhythm and grid;
- information-object grammar;
- material, light and colour temperature;
- sequence logic.

Cosmetic colour swaps or crop variations fail the divergence gate. Build one representative client-facing asset for every territory and use real headline/copy length.

## 4. Two Critique Passes

### Art-direction critique

Judge composition, typography, image specificity, craft, originality, rhythm and distance from generic templates. Ask:

- Does the asset have a visual decision that could only come from this brief?
- Could another client replace the logo with little consequence?
- Would an art director retain the strongest move after removing all decorative elements?
- Does the image carry evidence, rather than background atmosphere?

### Buyer-and-brand critique

Judge category literacy, credibility, proof, accessibility, commercial use and brand recognisability. Ask:

- Does a real buyer see the operating reality they need to trust?
- Can the client produce the world implied by the visual language?
- Does the material still support a concrete next action?
- Is the choice recognisably this client, this product and this channel?

Record critiques separately. A visually fashionable choice that fails buyer trust is rejected. A commercially correct template that fails art-direction critique is repaired before it becomes the design system.

## 5. Critical Thresholds

Score the selected territory using the visual benchmark. For a world-class target:

- total score at least `40 / 45`;
- composition, typography, imagery, visual idea and craft at least `4 / 5`;
- client-language uniqueness at least `4 / 5`;
- no unresolved objection from either critique pass.

The complete section also passes the visual-richness invariant from `visual-production-and-brand-assets.md`. A high-scoring cover cannot compensate for a repetitive or visually impoverished body sequence.

### Built-in quality before expansion

Before the second material or production wave, verify on the complete anchor section:

- final copy and proof remain visible at the intended scale;
- every page has a primary information object rather than distributed prose;
- the contact sheet shows a deliberate alternation of evidence, photographic, diagrammatic, comparative, editorial or tool/form pages according to content;
- no more than two neighbouring pages repeat the same layout grammar;
- client-specific operating reality remains recognisable after mentally removing the logo;
- the weakest body page, not only the cover or hero, meets the score floor.

Failure routes to `revise_current_iteration` or `replan_from_current_state` before batch production.

### Client-language uniqueness test

Remove the logo, company name and product labels mentally. Score:

| Score | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | the same page plausibly fits dozens of competitors |
| 3 | category and brand are present, but the composition remains conventional |
| 5 | the buyer can recognise the company’s operating world through evidence, materials, rhythm and proof logic |

## 6. Repair Loop

Use `revise_current_iteration` for a weak page inside an accepted direction. Use `replan_from_current_state` if the selected territory fails the critical thresholds. After each repair:

1. render the exact output surface;
2. compare it to the approved proposition and atlas;
3. record the observed visual change;
4. run both critiques again only on the affected dimension;
5. stop after two repairs without an observed improvement and request a human creative decision.

## 7. Learning Receipt

Store only reusable, evidence-led learning:

```markdown
## Art-direction learning receipt

Project and material type:
Reference units tested:
Creative proposition:
Territories rejected and why:
Selected mechanism:
Art-direction critique result:
Buyer-and-brand critique result:
Score before / after:
Human review received:
Observed effect after use:
Candidate reusable rule:
Counterexample or boundary:
Eval to add or update:
```

A client-specific choice stays project-local. Promote it into a shared rule only after varied projects, an explicit boundary and an eval fixture. `effect_verified` requires a fresh human review or use observation; a passing render confirms only surface quality.

Kaizen may raise the benchmark, add a tested page mode or improve a reference/critique method after observed review or use. It cannot weaken an existing gate, accept a generic template to preserve speed or treat output volume as proof of learning.

## Quality Failure Examples

- High-resolution AI photography combined with generic cards and default type scale.
- A reference board that lists attractive projects without stating the transferable unit.
- Three moodboards differing only by palette, crop or descriptive adjectives.
- A hero image with generated lettering, logo or impossible equipment.
- A technically clean deck where every slide repeats title, image and card positions.
- Art-direction review performed only after the full batch is assembled.
