# Reproducible Quality Floor

This reference captures cross-project safeguards learned from repeated production failures. It sets the minimum process for a new client programme while leaving client-specific aesthetics, facts and material choices local.

## The bounded production loop

For every new semantic material type:

1. approve the material job, reader, decision and same-job reference set;
2. approve the complete meaning plan and ABCD receipt;
3. write final client-facing copy and complete the claim-to-proof ledger;
4. build one representative anchor or complete short section;
5. inspect the real output surface, full sequence and weakest body page;
6. obtain the Human Gate named in the iteration contract;
7. scale only the accepted mechanics;
8. rerun protected invariants after every correction;
9. verify standalone files, cabinet, self-contained copy and publication surface;
10. collect first-use evidence before `effect_verified`.

An earlier approval of mood, cover, typography or another material does not approve a new semantic material type. Work created after the current Human Gate remains `internal_prototype`.

## Protected invariants after every repair

Recheck:

- reader, buyer situation, decision and next action;
- same-job reference receipt and accepted storyline;
- final-copy status and ABCD sequence;
- claim sources, freshness, qualifications and privacy;
- accepted visual proposition, visual rhythm and typography floor;
- image provenance, resolution, factual integrity and intended size;
- client-world naming and absence of internal labels or personal contacts;
- section boundaries, page counts, standalone export and publication parity;
- previously accepted decisions and the exact Human Gate boundary.

## Built-in quality checks

The anchor cannot advance while one of these conditions is present:

- adjacent document genres define the content order despite available same-job references;
- visible text describes production, methodology or future content instead of serving the reader;
- evidence is framed as an internal audit when the recipient needs the evidence itself;
- meaningful copy falls below the target-surface readability floor;
- text overlaps an image or leaves its intended colour field;
- blurred, undersized or contextually false imagery carries a proof role;
- the sequence collapses into repeated title-and-card compositions;
- a client-visible fact lacks a current source or relevant claim-to-proof link;
- a portal control, editor, download or navigation action has not been exercised;
- local, standalone and published versions differ in content, assets or behaviour;
- private team data, internal codes or unrelated project content remains visible.

## Human review and automatic QA

Automatic QA may verify geometry, page count, links, image loading, fonts, privacy patterns and cross-surface parity. Human review verifies meaning, audience fit, evidence interpretation, art direction and the readiness of a semantic material type. Record both statuses separately.

## Learning receipt

After a material correction, record:

```text
observed defect:
affected reader outcome:
correction route:
smallest repair:
fresh observation:
protected invariants rechecked:
human or use result:
local rule or shared candidate:
counterexample:
rollback route:
eval id:
```

Promote a cross-project rule only when the failure recurs across varied tasks or expresses a stable source, privacy, accessibility or functional invariant. Keep one client's aesthetic preference, terminology or page-count choice local.

## Counterexamples that prevent overfitting

- A four-page material may satisfy ABCD; ABCD does not prescribe four pages.
- A technical form can be the correct artifact when the reader job is data capture; it is the wrong primary archetype for a decision brief.
- Uniform components can support navigation and consistency; repeated page grammar becomes a defect when it hides different information objects.
- A testimonial can be strong Credentials for a service-experience claim; it is weak proof for a result it does not mention.
- A small caption may use a smaller type role; decision-bearing copy must remain readable at the intended surface and distance.
- A contact-only call to action can be sufficient for an already agreed operational handoff; decision-oriented materials need the first-step process.
